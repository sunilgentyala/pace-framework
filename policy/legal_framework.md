# PACE Framework — Legal Authorities and Constraints

## Governing Legal Framework

### Domestic Authorities

| Authority | Relevance to PACE |
|-----------|------------------|
| Title 10 U.S.C. (Armed Forces) | Governs USCYBERCOM offensive/defensive operations |
| Title 50 U.S.C. (War and National Defense) | Covert action authorities; intelligence community cyber operations |
| Posse Comitatus Act, 18 U.S.C. § 1385 | Limits military cyber engagement with domestic civilian infrastructure; DSCA pre-templates required |
| NDAA FY2025, §§ 1531-1534 | AI-assisted threat hunting pilot authorization |
| Presidential Policy Directives | NSC notification requirements for red/black category actions |

### International Law

| Framework | Relevance to PACE |
|-----------|------------------|
| UN Charter, Art. 2(4) | Prohibition on force; threshold analysis for Tier 3 actions |
| UN Charter, Art. 51 | Self-defense basis for Tier 1/2 autonomous actions |
| Tallinn Manual 2.0, Rule 6 | Due care standard; ANH actions must not affect third-party systems |
| Law of Armed Conflict (LOAC) | Proportionality and necessity requirements for all ANH tiers |
| Customary International Law | Attribution standards; state responsibility for cyber operations |

## Key Legal Arguments

### Tier 1 Autonomous Actions (Self-Defense Analogy)
Tier 1 ANH actions (block, quarantine, reroute) are legally analogous to
self-defense reflexes. They:
- Produce no effects outside the defended network
- Are proportionate to the observed threat
- Are reversible upon cessation of the threat
- Do not constitute "use of force" under any recognized international law framework

### Tier 2 Actions (Pre-Authorized Playbook)
Tier 2 actions require advance authorization at combatant command level and
a 90-second human window — satisfying the "meaningful human control" standard
increasingly referenced in international humanitarian law discussions of autonomous
defensive systems.

### DSCA Authority for Pillar C
Military cyber assistance to civilian infrastructure requires DSCA authority.
The PACE Framework's pre-crisis DSCA template approach:
1. Establishes authority before a crisis (not ad hoc)
2. Defines scope and limits of military assistance
3. Includes Posse Comitatus compliance measures
4. Requires appropriate legal review at the appropriate command level
