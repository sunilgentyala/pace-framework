# PACE Framework — Abstract

**Article:** Persistent Engagement at Machine Speed: The PACE Framework for Proactive,
AI-Driven Cyber-Physical Defense in Gray-Zone Conflict

**Author:** Sunil Gentyala (sunil.gentyala@ieee.org)

---

The 2026 threat environment has produced a convergence of conditions that existing
United States cyber defense doctrine was not designed to address simultaneously:
adversary automation operating at speeds no human analyst can match; gray-zone operations
calibrated to remain below the threshold of armed attack; and an expanding attack surface
created by the fusion of industrial control systems with military command-and-control
networks.

This article introduces the **Proactive, Artificial Intelligence-driven, Cyber-Physical,
Escalation-aware Defense (PACE) Framework** as a doctrinal architecture purpose-built for
this convergence. Across four reinforcing pillars, the framework integrates:

- Autonomous network healing governed by tiered rules of engagement
- An air-gapped large language model analytics pipeline to reduce false positives and normalize multi-domain intelligence artifacts
- A standardized mapping methodology linking civilian critical infrastructure resilience to military operational readiness
- A deterrence communication matrix designed to prevent escalation through miscalculation

**Keywords:** cyber defense doctrine; persistent engagement; artificial intelligence; critical infrastructure; escalation management
